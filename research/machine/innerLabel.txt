			Please note!
		Observe the operating instructions for the cipher machine (H. Dv; g. 13)

1. To clean the roller contacts, rotate all rollers forward and backward several times.
2. To clean the key contacts, press all keys down forcefully several times before turning on the power and let them snap up, keeping one key pressed at a time.
3. When setting the characters visible in the windows, ensure that the rollers are correctly engaged.
4. The non-interchangeable double-pin plugs must be inserted fully into their sockets. The front wooden flap must then be closed, otherwise three lamps may light up at the same time.
5. If no lamp lights up when the key is pressed, check the battery, its contact springs, its connections to the selector switch, and the selector switch.
6. If one or more lamps do not light up when a button is pressed, the corresponding lamps, the contacts beneath them, the cables of the double-pole plugs, the plug sockets including their shorting plates, the roller contacts, the make contacts beneath the currently pressed buttons, and the break contacts beneath the corresponding buttons must be checked and cleaned if any dirt or oxidation is present. (See also section 2).
	From machine no. A 4388 onwards, the opening on the right side of the lamp panel is used for lamp testing. From machine no. A 4388 onwards, the outermost left and right sockets in the middle row on the plug board and the cable test lamp on the left side of the lamp panel are used for cable testing.
7. The roller axle and roller bushings must be kept clean and, like all other bearings, lightly greased from time to time with resin- and acid-free oil. The fixed contacts of the rollers should be sanded with polishing paper every 6-8 weeks and wiped with a rag soaked in oil. The key contacts, the lamp contacts, and the short-circuit breaker should be protected from oil.
8. Key entries are given either as numbers or letters.
